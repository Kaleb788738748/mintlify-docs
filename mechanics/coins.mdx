Coins are Match & Cash's secondary in-game currency, displayed throughout the game as a gold coin icon. While coins aren't the cash rewards you're ultimately playing for, they're an important resource that gives you access to props — the power-up tools that can save a level when the shelves stop cooperating. Managing your coin balance well means having a safety net ready exactly when you need it.

## What Coins Are Used For

Coins have one primary purpose: purchasing props. Each of the three props — Recall One Good, Remove 3 Goods, and Rearrange All Goods — costs **100 coins** per use. If you'd rather not watch an ad in the moment, your coin balance lets you activate any prop instantly without interruption.

<Info>
  Coins are not the same as your cash reward balance. Coins stay inside the game and can only be spent on props. Your cash earnings are tracked separately and are redeemable via Cash App, PayPal, Amazon, Walmart, DoorDash, Target, and LEGO gift cards.
</Info>

## How to Earn Coins

### Watch Ads for +30 Coins

The fastest and most reliable way to earn coins is by watching short video ads. Each ad you complete rewards you with **+30 coins**, credited to your balance immediately after the ad finishes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the ad button">
    Look for the 🎬 button on the main game screen or the level-select screen. It's typically displayed alongside your current coin balance so you always know how close you are to your next prop purchase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap to watch">
    Tap the button to begin a short video ad. Ads typically run 15–30 seconds. Do not close the ad early, or the coins may not be credited.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect your +30 coins">
    Once the ad completes, 30 coins are added to your balance automatically. The updated total appears in the coin counter near the top of the screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  It takes **at least 4 ad views** to save up enough coins for one prop use (4 × 30 = 120 coins). Watching ads consistently between levels is the most effective way to maintain a healthy reserve.
</Note>

### Level Bonuses

Completing levels can also award bonus coins alongside your cash rewards. Bonus amounts vary by level difficulty and performance, but every little bit adds to your reserve.

<Tip>
  Even if a level feels easy and you don't need props, finishing it cleanly still contributes to your coin count through level bonuses. Don't skip easier levels when you're looking to stock up.
</Tip>

## How to Spend Coins

Coins are spent in a single context: activating a prop during gameplay. When you tap a prop icon at the bottom of the game screen, you're given the choice to spend 100 coins or watch a free ad instead.

| Action | Coin Cost |
|---|---|
| Recall One Good | 100 coins |
| Remove 3 Goods | 100 coins |
| Rearrange All Goods | 100 coins |

<Warning>
  Once you spend coins on a prop, the transaction is immediate and cannot be undone. Make sure you actually want to activate the prop before confirming the coin spend — especially for Rearrange All Goods, which changes the entire board layout instantly.
</Warning>

## Coin Strategy

<Accordion title="Save coins for genuinely difficult levels">
  Resist the temptation to spend coins freely on easier levels where patience alone could get you through. A reserve of 300–400 coins (enough for 3 prop uses) going into a hard level is far more valuable than clearing an easy level slightly faster.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Default to watching ads in-level when possible">
  Each prop can also be claimed free by watching an ad. If time allows and you're not in a high-pressure moment, choosing the ad option preserves your coin balance for situations where you need a prop instantly and can't afford to pause for an ad.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Watch ads proactively, not reactively">
  Don't wait until you're out of coins to start watching ads. Build a habit of tapping the 🎬 button between levels whenever it's available. Earning coins steadily in small increments of 30 keeps your balance topped up without requiring any single long ad session.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Track your balance before starting a hard level">
  Before diving into a level you expect to be challenging, check your coin total. If you have fewer than 100 coins, consider watching a few ads on the main screen first so you enter the level with at least one prop purchase ready to go.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Pairing the coin system with Daily Missions is a great strategy. Extended play sessions required for missions like "Play 120 minutes" give you plenty of time to watch ads in between levels and build up a significant coin reserve naturally. See the [Daily Missions guide](/mechanics/daily-missions) to learn more.
</Tip>
