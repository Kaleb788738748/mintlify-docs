Matching is the core skill you'll develop in Match & Cash. Every level places a set of goods across multiple shelves, and your goal is to tap those goods in the right order so that three identical items land in your tray at the same time — triggering an automatic clear and pushing you closer to completing the level and earning real cash rewards.

## The Shelf Structure

Goods are stacked on shelves arranged in rows. Not every item is available to tap right away — only goods on the **lowest accessible layer** of each shelf can be selected. Items sitting beneath others are locked until the goods on top of them are removed first.

<Info>
  Think of each shelf like a layered stack. You can only reach the items on the exposed surface. Clearing upper items reveals the goods buried underneath.
</Info>

This stacking system means level layouts are intentional puzzles. Rushing to tap the nearest items without thinking can bury the goods you actually need, making it harder to form matches later.

## The 7-Slot Tray

At the bottom of the screen sits your tray — a row of **7 slots** that holds the goods you've tapped off the shelves. When you tap a good, it flies down and occupies the next available slot from left to right, automatically sliding next to any goods of the same type already in the tray.

<Warning>
  If all 7 slots fill up before you complete a match, the level ends. Always keep an eye on how many open slots remain before tapping a new item.
</Warning>

When three identical goods occupy three consecutive slots, they flash and disappear automatically — freeing up those slots and adding to your match count. You never need to tap to confirm a match; it happens the moment the third matching item lands.

## How a Match Clears

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap a good on the shelf">
    Touch any accessible (un-stacked) item on a shelf. It animates down into your tray, filling the next open slot or sliding next to its matching counterparts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build toward three of a kind">
    Continue tapping goods strategically until three identical items occupy neighboring tray slots.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch the automatic clear">
    The moment a third matching good lands, all three items glow and vanish. Your tray opens up those three slots for new goods.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat until the shelf is empty">
    Keep matching until all goods are cleared from every shelf. Clearing all goods completes the level.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Progress Bar and Level Completion

On the right side of the screen, a **progress bar** tracks how many goods you've cleared relative to the total on the level. Each successful match of three items advances the bar. When the bar reaches 100%, the level is complete and your cash reward is credited to your balance.

<Tip>
  Levels don't require you to clear every single good in record time — but leaving your tray nearly full while the progress bar is only halfway done is a warning sign. Prioritize clearing tray space whenever you have two of the same item already waiting.
</Tip>

## Matching Strategy

Smart matching is the difference between breezing through a level and getting stuck with a full tray.

<Accordion title="Group same-type goods before tapping">
  Before tapping randomly, scan the visible shelf layer and identify which goods appear most frequently. Prioritize those items first — the more you move goods of the same type into the tray together, the faster you clear slots and maintain breathing room.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Don't chase rare goods too early">
  If you can only see one of a particular good, tapping it uses up a tray slot with no immediate match in sight. Hold off until you can see at least two of the same item accessible on the shelves before committing that slot.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Work from the top layers down">
  Clearing upper-shelf goods reveals what's underneath. Sometimes clearing one cluster on an upper shelf unlocks two or three goods beneath it that complete a match you already have waiting in the tray.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Monitor tray capacity constantly">
  At 5 or 6 slots filled with no immediate matches possible, it's time to reach for a prop rather than risk filling the last slot and losing the level. See the [Props guide](/mechanics/props) for when and how to use them.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  When you complete a level without using any props, you conserve coins for harder levels ahead. Aim for prop-free clears on easier stages to build up your coin reserve.
</Tip>
