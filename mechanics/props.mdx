Props are in-game power-up tools that give you a way out when a level turns tricky. Whether your tray is packed with mismatched goods, a shelf layout isn't cooperating, or you simply can't find the third item you need to clear a match, the right prop used at the right moment can rescue a level that looked like a loss. Every prop can be activated for **100 coins** or claimed **free by watching a short ad**.

## The Three Props

Each prop solves a different kind of problem. Understanding what each one does — and when to reach for it — is just as important as knowing how to match goods on the shelves.

| Prop | Effect | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Recall One Good** | Returns one item of your choice from the tray back to the shelf | 100 coins or free via ad |
| **Remove 3 Goods** | Instantly removes any 3 goods directly from the shelves | 100 coins or free via ad |
| **Rearrange All Goods** | Shuffles all remaining goods into new positions across all shelves | 100 coins or free via ad |

<Note>
  Each prop has a maximum of **3 uses per session**, displayed as a "Limit: 3/3" counter on the prop icon. Once you've used a prop three times in a session, it becomes unavailable for the rest of that session regardless of your coin balance.
</Note>

### Recall One Good

Recall One Good lets you send a single item from your tray back to the shelves, freeing up that slot. You choose exactly which item to recall — useful when one mismatched good is blocking your tray and preventing matches from forming.

<Tip>
  Use Recall when your tray has 6 or 7 items and you have a clear majority of goods you're close to matching, but one lone item is taking up space with no visible match in sight. Sending it back buys you critical room to finish your current matches.
</Tip>

### Remove 3 Goods

Remove 3 Goods reaches directly onto the shelves and eliminates three items instantly — no tray slot needed. You choose which shelf goods to target, so you can strategically clear a buried item that's blocking goods you need, or remove three mismatched strays you don't want cluttering the board.

<Tip>
  Remove 3 Goods is most powerful when used on a shelf's top layer to expose a cluster of matching goods underneath. Rather than removing random items, look for a spot where removing three goods creates a cascade of accessible matches.
</Tip>

### Rearrange All Goods

Rearrange All Goods shuffles every remaining good into new positions across all the shelves. The total number of goods doesn't change, but their locations and stacking order are completely randomized.

<Warning>
  Rearranging is a high-risk, high-reward move. It can break open a board that felt completely locked — but it can also scatter a favorable arrangement you had been building toward. Use it as a last resort when no other prop or strategy can unblock the level.
</Warning>

## How to Use a Prop

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the prop icons">
    The three prop icons sit at the bottom of the game screen during active gameplay. Each icon shows its name, a small visual, and the remaining use counter (e.g., 3/3).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the prop you want to use">
    Tap the prop icon to open its activation panel. You'll see two options: spend 100 coins immediately, or watch a short ad to claim it free.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your payment method">
    - **Spend coins** — tap the coin button to deduct 100 coins from your balance and activate the prop instantly.
    - **Watch an ad** — tap the **▶ Claim** button to play a short video ad. Once the ad completes, the prop activates at no coin cost.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply the prop effect">
    For **Recall** and **Remove**, you'll enter a selection mode — tap the specific good(s) you want to target. For **Rearrange**, the shuffle happens automatically across the entire board.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Prop Limits Explained

The counter on each prop icon (shown as **3/3** at full capacity) tells you how many uses remain for that prop in the current session. Each use — whether paid with coins or claimed via ad — decrements the counter by one.

<Info>
  Prop limits reset between sessions. If you exit the game and return later, your limits will be restored. The limit is per-session, not per-level, so use props conservatively if you're planning to play multiple levels in one sitting.
</Info>

## Coin vs. Ad: Which Should You Choose?

<Accordion title="When to watch the ad">
  If you have plenty of time and your coin balance is low, the free ad option is almost always the better choice. A 15–30 second ad preserves 100 coins you may need later in a harder level where a prop is urgently required.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="When to spend coins">
  If you're in the middle of a level and can't afford to break your focus — or if you've already watched several ads and prefer not to watch another — spending 100 coins is a fast, seamless option. See the [Coins guide](/mechanics/coins) for how to build up your coin reserve efficiently.
</Accordion>

## Strategic Tips

<Accordion title="Save props for levels near the end of your tray space">
  Props are most valuable when you're one or two taps away from a full tray. A Recall or Remove used at that moment can extend the level; the same prop used when you have 4 open tray slots is less critical.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Use Remove 3 Goods to solve bottlenecks, not just reduce count">
  Don't remove goods at random. Find three items that are blocking access to a cluster of goods you need, and remove those. This creates a multiplier effect — one prop use unlocks several future matches.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Treat Rearrange as an emergency reset">
  If you've used Recall and Remove and the board still feels unwinnable, Rearrange gives you a fresh layout. Accept that you're gambling on a better random distribution and commit to the decision rather than saving it indefinitely.
</Accordion>
