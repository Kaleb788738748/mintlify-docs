Winning at Match & Cash isn't just about tapping fast — it's about tapping smart. The difference between a cleared board and a jammed tray often comes down to a handful of deliberate decisions: which item you tap next, when you deploy a prop, and how you pace your daily sessions. Use this guide to sharpen every layer of your strategy and keep the cash flowing.

<Accordion title="Tray Management">
  Your 7-slot tray is the most valuable resource on the board. Once it fills up with unmatched goods, the game is effectively over unless you spend a prop. Every tap should be made with your current tray state in mind.

  <Warning>
    Never tap a brand-new type of good unless you already have 2 matching items in your tray **or** you have at least 2–3 free slots to absorb the risk. Introducing a lone item of a new type eats a slot with no immediate payoff.
  </Warning>

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Audit your tray before every tap">
      Glance at the tray first. Count how many distinct types are currently sitting there and how many open slots remain.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Prioritize completions">
      If you already have 2 of a kind in the tray, your next tap should be the matching third item — even if you have to hunt for it on the shelf.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Clear pairs before adding new types">
      Resist the urge to grab interesting-looking goods. Finish the pair you started, clear the three, and only then introduce a new type with the freed-up slots.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Keep a buffer">
      Try to keep at least 2 slots free at all times. A full or near-full tray removes all flexibility and forces expensive prop use.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  <Tip>
    If you find yourself with 5 or more items in the tray and no immediate matches in sight, use **Recall One Good** to return the most "isolated" item (the one with no siblings in the tray) back to the shelf before things get critical.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Shelf Reading">
  Before you tap anything, spend a few seconds reading the board. Shelves are stacked — lower items are locked until the goods above them are removed. A little upfront planning saves a lot of prop spending later.

  <Note>
    Items on lower shelf rows are only accessible once the items stacked on top of them have been cleared. Always think at least two "layers" ahead.
  </Note>

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Spot clusters of the same type">
      Scan every visible row for goods that share an icon. Identify which shelf column holds the most matchable cluster and plan to work that column first.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Map your unlock path">
      Determine which upper-layer items you need to remove to expose the goods you actually want. Sometimes tapping an upper item you don't need is worth it if it unlocks three of a kind below.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Prioritize money bundle items">
      Money bundle goods on the shelves count as regular matchable items **and** give a direct cash bonus when you collect a matching trio. Always prefer clearing money bundle clusters over equivalent non-cash items when your tray allows it.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Avoid orphan taps">
      An "orphan tap" is grabbing a good when there's only one of its type visible on the entire board. Unless you can see the other two hiding behind unlockable layers, skip it.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  <Tip>
    Rotate your attention across all shelves each round rather than fixating on one column. A match you missed on the far-right shelf might be exactly what saves your tray.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Prop Timing">
  Props are powerful, but players who hoard them often end up losing levels they could have won. Each prop has a specific sweet spot — learn to recognize those moments and deploy with confidence.

  <Note>
    Every prop costs **100 coins** or can be claimed free by watching a short ad. Because coins are replenishable (30 per ad), there's no reason to sit on a pile of unused props while a level falls apart. Keep in mind that each prop type can be used a **maximum of 3 times per session** — so pace your usage across a session rather than burning through all three early on.
  </Note>

  **Rearrange All Goods**

  Reshuffles the entire shelf layout. Use this when:
  - The board looks completely stuck with no clear path to any match.
  - You've worked through most of a level but a few stubborn goods are scattered with no obvious cluster.

  <Warning>
    Don't use Rearrange All Goods on a nearly full tray — the new layout won't help you if you can't tap anything new anyway. Clear your tray first, then rearrange.
  </Warning>

  **Remove 3 Goods**

  Removes 3 goods directly from shelves. Use this when:
  - Three specific items are physically blocking access to a cluster you need below them.
  - You want to expose a money bundle trio that's one layer down.

  **Recall One Good**

  Returns one item from your tray back to the shelf. Use this when:
  - You accidentally tapped the wrong item and your tray is getting dangerously full.
  - You have a lone item of a rare type taking up a slot with no matches in sight.

  <Tip>
    Claim the free prop from an ad **before** you need it desperately. If you see a "watch ad for free prop" offer and your tray has 4+ items, take it now rather than waiting until you're at 7.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Daily Mission Strategy">
  Daily Missions offer some of the best cash-to-effort ratios in the game. A single completed mission (like "play for 120 minutes → earn $5") can outpace multiple individual level completions. Approach them with a plan.

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Check missions first thing">
      Open the Daily Missions panel at the start of each session. Knowing the day's targets lets you structure your play time around them rather than discovering a 120-minute goal with 10 minutes left before midnight.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Pace time-based missions">
      If a mission requires 120 minutes of play, split it across 2–3 sessions throughout the day (e.g., 45 min morning, 45 min afternoon, 30 min evening). You don't have to grind it all at once.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Stack missions with normal play">
      Most missions align naturally with regular gameplay (complete levels, collect matches, etc.). Simply playing normally will tick off multiple missions at the same time — no special mode needed.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Claim before midnight">
      Daily Missions reset at midnight. If you've completed a mission, claim the reward **before** the reset — unclaimed completed missions do not carry over.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  <Warning>
    Forgetting to claim a completed Daily Mission before midnight means losing that reward permanently. Make it a habit to check the missions panel at the end of your evening session.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Coin Efficiency">
  Coins are the fuel for props, and props are what keep your win streak alive. Fortunately, coins are easy to earn — the key is spending them at the right moments.

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Watch one ad whenever you have a spare moment">
      Each ad earns you +30 coins. Three ads = 90 coins, almost a full prop. Build a habit of watching one ad between levels while the results screen is up.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Always take the free prop offer">
      When the game offers a free prop for watching an ad, accept it even if you don't think you need it right now. Free props save your coin balance for when things get truly difficult.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Save coins for high-stakes moments">
      Don't spend coins on a prop just because a level feels slightly tricky. Save them for late-stage levels where the tray jams frequently and a single prop can mean the difference between finishing or not.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Replenish after spending">
      After you spend coins on a prop, queue up a couple of ads in your next natural break to restore your balance. Don't let your coin count hit zero — arriving at a tough level with 0 coins is a stressful position.
    </Step>
  </Steps>

  <Tip>
    Think of your coin balance as an emergency fund: keep it above 200 at all times (enough for 2 props) and actively top it up with ad watches so you're never caught short.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>
